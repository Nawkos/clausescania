EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

("Employer" or "Company")

and

EMPLOYEE: Sarah Williams

("Employee")

The parties agree as follows:

1. POSITION AND DUTIES

1.1 Position
The Employee is employed as Senior Software Engineer, reporting to the Head of Engineering.

1.2 Duties and Responsibilities
The Employee shall:
   a) Design, develop, and maintain software applications as directed
   b) Participate in code reviews and technical planning meetings
   c) Mentor junior developers as appropriate
   d) Contribute to the improvement of development processes
   e) Perform other duties as reasonably assigned and consistent with the position

1.3 Standard of Performance
The Employee shall perform duties diligently, professionally, and to the best of their ability.

1.4 Policies and Procedures
The Employee agrees to comply with all Company policies, procedures, and lawful directions, as may be updated from time to time.

2. TERM OF EMPLOYMENT

2.1 Commencement
Employment commences on December 1, 2025.

2.2 Probationary Period
The first three (3) months shall constitute a probationary period during which either party may terminate employment with one (1) week's written notice.

2.3 Continuing Employment
Upon successful completion of the probationary period, employment continues indefinitely subject to the terms of this Agreement.

3. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

3.1 Base Salary
The Employee shall receive an annual salary of £65,000, payable in equal monthly installments on the last business day of each month, subject to applicable tax withholdings.

3.2 Salary Review
The Company will review the Employee's salary annually, typically in January. Any increase is at the Company's discretion based on performance and market conditions.

3.3 Performance Bonus
The Employee is eligible to participate in the Company's annual performance bonus scheme, with a target bonus of 10% of base salary. Actual bonus amount depends on Company and individual performance and is paid in February following the performance year.

3.4 Pension Scheme
The Company will contribute to the Employee's workplace pension in accordance with UK auto-enrolment requirements. Currently, the Company contributes 5% of salary if the Employee contributes at least 3%.

3.5 Health Insurance
The Company provides private health insurance for the Employee through Bupa, including dental and optical coverage. Coverage begins on the Start Date.

3.6 Life Insurance
The Company provides life insurance coverage equal to four times annual salary at no cost to the Employee.

3.7 Stock Options
Subject to Board approval, the Employee will be granted options to purchase 10,000 shares of Company stock at the current fair market value. Options vest over four years: 25% after one year, then monthly thereafter. Full details are in the Stock Option Agreement.

3.8 Professional Development
The Company provides an annual professional development budget of £2,000 for conferences, courses, and certifications relevant to the Employee's role.

4. WORKING HOURS AND LOCATION

4.1 Standard Hours
Normal working hours are 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM, Monday through Friday, with one hour for lunch (37.5 hours per week).

4.2 Flexible Working
The Company operates flexible working hours. Employees may adjust start/end times with manager approval, provided core hours (10 AM - 3 PM) are covered.

4.3 Remote Work
The Employee may work remotely up to two (2) days per week with manager approval. The Company will provide necessary equipment for remote work.

4.4 Primary Workplace
The Employee's primary workplace is the Company's Cambridge office, though business needs may occasionally require work at other locations.

4.5 Overtime
As a salaried employee, the position is exempt from overtime pay. However, the Company expects working hours to be reasonable and will monitor workload to ensure a healthy work-life balance.

5. HOLIDAY AND LEAVE

5.1 Annual Holiday
The Employee is entitled to 25 days of paid holiday per year, plus eight UK bank holidays (33 days total).

5.2 Holiday Accrual
Holiday entitlement accrues monthly. During the first year, entitlement is prorated.

5.3 Holiday Scheduling
Holiday must be requested through the Company's system with reasonable advance notice (typically two weeks for short absences, four weeks for absences exceeding one week). Approval is subject to business needs.

5.4 Holiday Carryover
Up to five (5) unused holiday days may be carried over to the next year, with manager approval. Carried-over days must be used by March 31.

5.5 Statutory Leave
The Employee is entitled to statutory leave as provided by UK law, including:
   a) Sick leave (with Statutory Sick Pay after three days)
   b) Maternity/Paternity leave
   c) Parental leave
   d) Bereavement leave (five paid days for immediate family)

5.6 Company Enhanced Leave
In addition to statutory entitlements, the Company provides:
   a) Full salary for first 10 days of sick leave per year (then SSP)
   b) Enhanced maternity/paternity pay (12 weeks full pay, 14 weeks half pay)
   c) Three paid days for moving house
   d) Paid time off for medical appointments

6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

6.1 Work Product Ownership
All work product, inventions, and intellectual property created by the Employee during employment and within the scope of employment duties shall be the property of the Company.

6.2 Definition of Work Product
"Work product" includes all software code, designs, documentation, processes, and other materials created for the Company or using Company resources.

6.3 Assignment
The Employee agrees to execute any documents necessary to confirm the Company's ownership of work product.

6.4 Pre-Existing IP
Intellectual property created by the Employee before employment or outside of employment duties using personal resources remains the Employee's property, provided it does not:
   a) Relate to the Company's business or actual or demonstrably anticipated research
   b) Result from work performed for the Company
   c) Use Company confidential information or resources

6.5 Disclosure of Prior IP
The Employee has disclosed any pre-existing intellectual property that might be relevant to their employment (see Appendix A, or none if not applicable).

6.6 Open Source Contribution
The Employee may contribute to open-source projects outside working hours with manager approval, provided contributions do not conflict with Company interests or use Company confidential information.

7. CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1 Confidential Information
The Employee acknowledges access to Company confidential information, including business strategies, customer data, source code, financial information, and trade secrets.

7.2 Non-Disclosure Obligation
The Employee agrees to:
   a) Maintain confidentiality of all Company confidential information
   b) Use confidential information only for Company business purposes
   c) Not disclose confidential information to third parties without authorization
   d) Return all confidential materials upon termination

7.3 Exceptions
These obligations do not apply to information that:
   a) Is publicly available through no fault of the Employee
   b) Must be disclosed by law (provided the Employee gives advance notice to the Company if legally permitted)
   c) Is independently developed by the Employee without using Company confidential information

7.4 Survival
Confidentiality obligations survive termination of employment indefinitely.

8. DATA PROTECTION

8.1 Employee Data
The Company processes Employee personal data in accordance with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. Details are in the Employee Privacy Notice.

8.2 Customer Data
In the course of employment, the Employee may process customer or other third-party personal data. The Employee agrees to comply with all applicable data protection laws and Company data protection policies.

9. TERMINATION

9.1 Notice Period - Post Probation
After the probationary period, either party may terminate employment by giving three (3) months' written notice.

9.2 Company Right to Pay in Lieu
The Company may elect to pay salary in lieu of all or part of the notice period (garden leave).

9.3 Summary Dismissal
The Company may terminate employment immediately without notice or payment in lieu for gross misconduct, including but not limited to:
   a) Serious breach of Company policies
   b) Theft or fraud
   c) Serious negligence
   d) Conduct bringing the Company into disrepute
   e) Material breach of this Agreement

9.4 Return of Property
Upon termination, the Employee must immediately return all Company property, including laptop, phone, access cards, and documents.

9.5 Post-Termination Obligations
Obligations regarding confidentiality, intellectual property, and non-disparagement survive termination.

10. RESTRICTIVE COVENANTS

10.1 Non-Compete - Limited and Reasonable
For six (6) months following termination, the Employee agrees not to work for a direct competitor in a similar role, where "direct competitor" means a company offering substantially similar products/services to the Company's core business in the UK market.

Note: This restriction is limited in scope and duration to protect legitimate business interests while allowing the Employee reasonable career mobility.

10.2 Non-Solicitation of Employees
For twelve (12) months following termination, the Employee agrees not to solicit or recruit Company employees for employment elsewhere.

10.3 Non-Solicitation of Customers
For twelve (12) months following termination, the Employee agrees not to solicit Company customers with whom they had material contact during the final twelve months of employment.

10.4 Reasonableness
These restrictions are designed to be reasonable and necessary to protect legitimate business interests. If any restriction is deemed too broad, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to be enforceable.

11. GRIEVANCE AND DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES

11.1 Grievance Procedure
If the Employee has a grievance related to employment, they should first raise it with their manager. If unresolved, formal grievance procedures are outlined in the Employee Handbook.

11.2 Disciplinary Procedure
Any disciplinary matters will be handled in accordance with the Company's disciplinary procedure and UK employment law, including ACAS guidelines.

11.3 Right to Representation
The Employee has the right to be accompanied by a colleague or trade union representative at formal grievance or disciplinary meetings.

12. OUTSIDE ACTIVITIES

12.1 Conflicts of Interest
The Employee must avoid conflicts of interest and disclose any situations that might create a conflict, including:
   a) Outside employment
   b) Board positions
   c) Significant investments in competitors
   d) Personal relationships with suppliers/customers

12.2 Outside Employment
The Employee may engage in outside employment or consulting with advance written approval from the Company, provided it does not:
   a) Interfere with Employee's duties
   b) Compete with Company business
   c) Use Company resources or confidential information

13. HEALTH AND SAFETY

13.1 Company Obligations
The Company will provide a safe working environment and comply with all health and safety regulations.

13.2 Employee Obligations
The Employee must:
   a) Comply with health and safety policies
   b) Report hazards or incidents
   c) Use safety equipment as required
   d) Take reasonable care for their own safety and that of others

14. EQUAL OPPORTUNITIES

14.1 Company Policy
The Company is an equal opportunity employer and does not discriminate on the basis of age, disability, gender, race, religion, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic.

14.2 Harassment and Bullying
The Company has zero tolerance for harassment, bullying, or discrimination. Such conduct may result in disciplinary action up to and including dismissal.

15. CHANGES TO TERMS

15.1 Modification
The Company may make reasonable changes to employment terms by giving one month's written notice. Significant changes require the Employee's consent.

15.2 Employee Handbook
This Agreement should be read in conjunction with the Employee Handbook, which may be updated from time to time. The Employee will be notified of material changes.

16. GENERAL PROVISIONS

16.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement, together with the Employee Handbook and any referenced documents, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding employment.

16.2 Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales.

16.3 Jurisdiction
The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

16.4 Severability
If any provision is found to be unenforceable, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary, and the remainder of the Agreement remains in force.

16.5 Notices
Notices must be in writing and delivered to the addresses listed above or such other address as either party may designate in writing.

17. ACCEPTANCE
