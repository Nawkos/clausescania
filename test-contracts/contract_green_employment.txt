ARTICLE 1 - POSITION AND DUTIES
1.1 The Employee is hired for the position of Senior Product Manager.
1.2 The Employee will report directly to the Chief Product Officer.
1.3 Primary responsibilities include:
    - Leading product strategy and roadmap development
    - Managing cross-functional teams
    - Analyzing market trends and user feedback
    - Collaborating with engineering and design teams
1.4 Duties may evolve with mutual agreement to support business needs and career development.

ARTICLE 2 - EMPLOYMENT TYPE AND START DATE
2.1 This is a permanent employment contract (CDI) under French law.
2.2 Start date: December 1, 2025
2.3 Probationary period: 4 months, renewable once by mutual written agreement for an additional 4 months.
2.4 During probation, either party may terminate with notice: 
    - 24 hours for first 8 days
    - 48 hours from day 8 to end of first month
    - 2 weeks thereafter
    - 1 month if probation renewed

ARTICLE 3 - COMPENSATION
3.1 Annual gross salary: €65,000, paid in 12 equal monthly installments.
3.2 Payment by bank transfer on the last business day of each month.
3.3 Annual salary review based on performance and company results.
3.4 Performance bonus: Up to 15% of annual salary based on achievement of agreed objectives (reviewed quarterly).

ARTICLE 4 - BENEFITS
4.1 Health insurance: Company provides complementary health insurance (mutuelle) covering 100% of premiums for Employee and 50% for dependents.
4.2 Meal vouchers: €10 per working day (60% employer contribution).
4.3 Transportation: 50% reimbursement of public transport pass (Navigo).
4.4 Professional development: €2,000 annual budget for training, conferences, or certifications.
4.5 Remote work: 2 days per week, with flexibility for additional remote days upon manager approval.
4.6 Company equipment: Laptop, phone, and necessary work tools provided and maintained by Company.

ARTICLE 5 - WORKING TIME
5.1 39 hours per week (full-time), Monday to Friday.
5.2 Schedule: Flexible within core hours of 10 AM - 4 PM; specific schedule agreed with manager.
5.3 RTT (Reduction of Working Time): 22 days per year in addition to paid leave.
5.4 Overtime compensated per French labor law and collective agreement.

ARTICLE 6 - PAID LEAVE
6.1 Annual paid leave: 25 working days (5 weeks) per full year of service, accrued monthly.
6.2 Additional leave: Per collective agreement and French law (events, special circumstances).
6.3 Leave requests submitted at least 2 weeks in advance for approval.
6.4 Unused leave may be carried over per legal requirements.

ARTICLE 7 - INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
7.1 Work created by Employee in the course of employment belongs to the Company.
7.2 Employee retains rights to pre-existing intellectual property brought to the role.
7.3 For inventions qualifying under French law, Employee entitled to fair compensation beyond salary if significant commercial value.
7.4 Employee may reference work experience and projects in professional portfolio with Company approval (not to disclose confidential information).

ARTICLE 8 - CONFIDENTIALITY
8.1 Employee agrees to maintain confidentiality of proprietary business information during and after employment.
8.2 This includes: business strategies, customer data, financial information, and unreleased products.
8.3 Obligation continues for 3 years after employment ends.
8.4 Employee may disclose information if required by law or court order, with prior notice to Company when possible.

ARTICLE 9 - NON-COMPETE
9.1 For 6 months following termination, Employee agrees not to work directly for companies in direct competition within the same product category.
9.2 Geographic restriction: Paris metropolitan area.
9.3 Compensation: Company pays 50% of last gross monthly salary for non-compete period.
9.4 Company may waive this clause upon termination by written notice, releasing Employee immediately.
9.5 If Company does not pay compensation, clause becomes void.

ARTICLE 10 - TERMINATION
10.1 Either party may terminate with written notice per French law:
    - By Employee: 3 months notice
    - By Employer: Follows legal notice periods based on seniority and collective agreement
10.2 Notice period may be reduced by mutual written agreement.
10.3 Termination must comply with French Labor Code and applicable collective agreements.
10.4 Severance pay per French law for dismissal without serious misconduct.

ARTICLE 11 - PROFESSIONAL EXPENSES
11.1 Company reimburses reasonable business expenses upon submission of receipts.
11.2 This includes: client meetings, business travel, professional materials.
11.3 Expense reports submitted monthly through company system.

ARTICLE 12 - DATA PROTECTION

12.2 Employee has rights to access, rectify, and request deletion of personal data.
12.3 Data retained only as long as necessary for employment and legal obligations.

ARTICLE 13 - HEALTH AND SAFETY
13.1 Company provides a safe working environment per French regulations.
13.2 Employee must report any workplace hazards or accidents immediately.
13.3 Regular health and safety training provided.

ARTICLE 14 - ETHICAL STANDARDS

14.2 Whistleblower protection available for reporting violations in good faith.

ARTICLE 15 - MODIFICATIONS

ARTICLE 16 - COLLECTIVE AGREEMENT

16.2 More favorable provisions of collective agreement supersede this contract.
